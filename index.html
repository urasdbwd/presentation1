Use privacy focused apps.

Many companies track your activities without your knowledge. They track you across websites, monitor what you do, and sell your trace to advertisers.

Chat privately

When engaging in online communication, it's crucial to ensure that your conversations remain private and secure. In today's digital age, where data breaches and surveillance are increasingly common, taking steps to protect your privacy is more important than ever. You wouldn't want a company or unauthorized third parties to have access to your personal conversations or see what you're doing online. This includes not only the content of your messages but also any shared files, photos, or sensitive information.

Search Privately

Using a privacy-focused search engine is essential if you don't want your data to be sold. A search engine prevents a lot of trackers from Google or Microsoft sending you personalized ads to sell your attention.

Connect Privately

Using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) is an effective strategy for safeguarding your online privacy, particularly from your Internet Service Provider (ISP). ISPs have the capability to track and log your online activities, which can include the websites you visit, the content you access, and even the messages you send. This data collection not only raises privacy concerns but can also lead to targeted advertising or, in some cases, the selling of your data to third parties. By using a VPN, your internet traffic is encrypted and routed through a secure server, masking your IP address and making your online actions much harder to track. This encryption ensures that your ISP cannot easily see what websites you're visiting or what data you're transmitting. See more on the VPNs page where we talk about the best vpns to use.

Additional Privacy Strategies

1. Avoid Free Services

Steer clear of free online services. Remember, if a service is free, you might be the product, as your data could be collected or sold.

2. Conceal Your IP Address

Use a VPN to hide your IP address, especially when connected to public Wi-Fi networks or while visiting websites of dubious security. Your ISP can monitor all your traffic, so use a VPN so they can't.

3. Use a better browser

Use a browser such as Brave, Brave prevents trackers and blocks ads.

4. Use different search engines

Don't use Google. They track and sell your search data, try using DuckDuckGo and Google only when needed.
